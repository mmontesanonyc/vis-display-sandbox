Notes on this approach:

  • Some basic functionality works, but plenty of buttons don't - they're just stand-ins
  • Use a CHS indicator with 4 responses (can we simplify further for yes/no questions?)
  • Remove particularly complex functions: compare two indicators; test statistical significance
  • Give a logical structure (overview, time, neighborhood, groups)
  • Add some user-controlled display options, like Show CIs, or Bar/Heatmap (in Groups)
  • Big question: what's the best 'overview' landing display?
  • Big question: what views to show together, to add context or detail? Trend, doing by-borough small multiples; in Neighborhood, also adding a Citywide trend, and a click-to-add-neighborhood trend. Too much?
  • This is more like current EQ displays that focus on a single indicators, compared to an EHDP display that lets you toggle indicators that share a common topic. Brings up Unit Of Organization question.
  • Do we want a table view?
  • We may consider metadata or conditions for value-judgement answers: eg is yes good or is no good? is there a sequential nature to categorical responses?
  • What sort of mutual interaction do we want - eg, clicking on a visualization renders a change elsewhere... best to limit these to buttons?
